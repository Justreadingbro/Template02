About

The Story Behind
the Edit

I'm Maya Chen — a cinematic editor based in Los Angeles with over a decade of experience shaping stories for screen. My work sits at the intersection of narrative instinct and technical precision, serving brands, agencies, and independent filmmakers who demand more than a cut.

I've had the privilege of editing campaigns for Fortune 500 companies, music videos for Grammy-nominated artists, and documentaries that screened at Sundance and TIFF. Every project, regardless of scale, starts the same way: listening to what the story needs.

My approach is hands-on and collaborative. I work directly with directors, creative directors, and producers from first assembly through color grade — ensuring the editorial vision survives every stage of post-production.

— Maya Chen

Process

How We Work Together

A structured but flexible editorial workflow built for creative collaboration at every stage.

Week 1

Discovery & Brief

We talk through the project goals, creative references, deadlines, and deliverables. I learn your story.

Week 1–2

Assembly & Selects

I review all footage, log selects, and build a first assembly that captures the narrative spine.

Week 2–3

Fine Cut & Revisions

Collaborative review cycles. We refine pacing, trim fat, and strengthen every story beat together.

Week 3–4

Grade, Sound & Delivery

Color grading, sound design, final mix, and mastering. Delivered in any format you need.

FAQ

Common Questions

I work primarily in Avid Media Composer for long-form and Premiere Pro for commercial projects. For color, I use DaVinci Resolve Studio with an ACES workflow. I'm happy to adapt to your pipeline.
I include three revision rounds in every project. I use Frame.io for collaborative review — you leave time-stamped feedback, I turn it around within 24–48 hours depending on scope.
A :30 commercial: 5–7 business days for first cut. A 3-minute music video: 10–14 days. Feature documentaries: 8–12 weeks. Rush delivery is available at a premium — reach out to discuss.
I'm based in Los Angeles and prefer working remotely from my studio. For larger productions, I'm available for on-site sessions in LA and NYC. I can also supervise remote editorial teams.
Anything from ProRes 4444 and DNxHR for broadcast to H.264/H.265 for digital. I also deliver DCPs for cinema, broadcast-spec masters, and social-platform optimized exports. All with proper LUTs baked in.
